Nipple Care:

Callen Lorde's surgery doula said to Keep ‘em moist, don’t worry if they get scabby, they are supposed to, and follow surgeon's directions. Disclaimer: A thin layer of bacitracin or vaseline. Not TOO moist. I'll be adding a video or photo soon to show the right amount to put on, because it shouldn't be left open to interpretation.:

  • Xeroform Petrolatum Gauze, Sterile, 2" W x 2"
    If you are allergic to some metal composites here's the alternative: ADAPTIC

  • Gauze 4 x 4
    You'll fold into a 2 X 2 square and place over Xeroform.

  • Tape 2 x 2
    To secure gauze to skin.

  • Bacitracin
    If you are allergic, use Vaseline instead, but buy a new one dedicated only to your nipples and only put the Q-tips in it to keep things sanitary.

  • Q-tips

  • Gloves

  • Alcohol Prep Pads

  • Surgical Scissors

  • Body Glide Anti Chafing Stick OR Gold Bond Friction Defense Stick

Scar Care

Always test on a small patch of skin before putting on your scar.

  • Silicone Tape
    Most people, including research agree silicone tape is the best for this kind of scar treatment. It both hydrates and helps keep the scar from stretching.

  • Kelo-Cote
    is another option. It's a gel that dries into a silicone sheet.

  • Alternatives other folx use: Mederma, Bio-Oil, Coconut Oil or Jojoba Oil.

Sleep and Comfort

None of the things listed are necessary. You just need to find a way to not roll onto your chest the first month. That's important, however you do it. Same with compression:

  • Zero-Gravity Chair (29 inches wide) If you toss and turn in bed, or sleep on your chest this will prevent that while keeping your back healthy and torso elevated while healing. It's worth having the pad for comfort and tray for all the water you'll be drinking. Here's the X-large 33inch wide version

  • Wedge pillow

  • Neck Pillow

  • Pregnancy Pillow

  • 14.5 inch Straws
    To make drinking easier the first week or so.

  • Mastectomy Pillow
    It's extra protection in the car if someone hits the brakes, and at a crowded airport signals people to stay the fuck away. It's the human version of a dog's cone of shame. It comes with an icepack which I used multiple times a day. If you drive the seatbelt pad is great. You could get any of these seperately as well, or just use a pillow if you want a cheaper option.
